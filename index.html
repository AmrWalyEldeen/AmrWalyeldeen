About Me

I am an Assistant Lecturer and researcher at the Department of Zoology, Faculty of Science, Cairo University. My research interests span cancer biology, extracellular vesicles, tumor microenvironment, and translational oncology. I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. focusing on proteomic profiling of circulating exosomes from breast cancer patients to identify exosomal proteome signatures as non-invasive, cost-effective biomarkers for early detection and disease monitoring.

Running Research Projects

Extracellular vesicle-derived proteoglycans as non-invasive liquid biopsy-based cancer biomarkers: Towards novel breast cancer screening (Project No. 46282)

Role: Research team member (Researcher B, M.Sc. holder)

Funding: Science, Technology & Innovation Funding Authority (STDF)

Budget: 1,991,170 LE   |   Duration: 2 years

This project develops cost-effective methodologies for isolating exosomes and identifying non-invasive biomarkers to improve early breast cancer detection. The outcomes aim to serve hospitals, cancer centers, biotech and diagnostic companies, and health authorities, and support commercialization and collaboration between academia and industry.

Resulting manuscripts (under review):
• Identification of Cathepsin L and Urokinase Type Plasminogen Activator in plasma-derived extracellular vesicles of breast cancer patients. (Submitted to Scientific Reports).
• Plasma exosomal proteomic signature in neoadjuvant chemotherapy–naïve breast cancer patients. (Submitted to PLOS ONE).

Proteomic profiling of exosomes: Discovery of novel diagnostic biomarkers for lymph node metastasis in breast cancer (Project No. Respect1-10052)

Role: Research team member (Researcher B, M.Sc. holder)

Funding: Academy of Scientific Research & Technology (ASRT)

Budget: 1,490,000 LE   |   Duration: 2 years

This project focuses on proteomic profiling of exosomes to discover diagnostic biomarkers for detecting lymph node metastasis in breast cancer. It led to a low-cost PEG-based exosome isolation protocol for resource-limited clinical labs, and provided training in proteomics and bioinformatics through collaborations with the National Cancer Institute and Baheya Center.

Resulting publications:
• Talat, L.Y. et al. “SDC2 and FN as cargo proteins in circulating extracellular vesicles in obese breast cancer patients with lymph node metastasis.” Scientific Reports 15(1): 32498, 2025.
• Exosomal Protease Profiling Identifies elevated latent MMP8 in Breast Cancer Patients with Lymph Node Metastasis. (Under review).
